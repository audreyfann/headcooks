How it combines

  • Same ingredient, different words — “Baby spinach”, “fresh spinach”, “Spinach” all group together.
  • Same ingredient, different units — flour in cups and flour in grams are summed into one total, using standard staple densities (and a rough estimate, flagged “~est”, for everything else).
  • Garlic is converted entirely into cloves, whatever unit it started in.
  • Sorted by store — learned from the spreadsheet's Supplier column. Items with no supplier history land in “Unassigned”; pick a store from the dropdown to teach it for next time.
  • Nothing leaves your browser — the spreadsheet is parsed locally, and the store you pick for each ingredient is remembered locally too (this browser, this device), so it's ready next time you upload a new spreadsheet.

All processing happens in your browser — nothing is uploaded. Store assignments you make are saved in this browser's local storage so they carry over to future uploads on this device; they won't follow you to a different browser or computer. Values for garlic, staple densities, and the fallback weight/volume estimate are standard kitchen-reference approximations — good for shopping, not lab-precise.
